What is the movement cost for an infantry squad to enter a woods hex from open ground?
A.
Entering a Woods hex costs 2 MF for non-Cavalry infantry, regardless of the hexside crossed (B13.4). If the move begins or ends on a road, the road rate B3.4 does not apply because Woods overrides road effects.
01 · The domain

A game where looking things up
takes as long as playing.

Advanced Squad Leader is a tactical WWII wargame played on hex maps with cardboard counters and dice. Most play has moved online through VASSAL and VASL — but those platforms only move counters and roll dice. Every rule interpretation, modifier, and edge case still falls on the players.

And the rules are dense. A single firefight can require cross-referencing terrain effects, line-of-sight rules, morale modifiers, and a dozen other sub-systems before a single die is rolled. The 716-page rulebook is the bottleneck.

The assistant supports two recurring tasks

  1. 01 Rule lookup — no-calculation answers, e.g. "What is the MF cost to enter a woods hex?"
  2. 02 Outcome calculation — given a board state, what DR resolves an effect? "What roll do I need to break that squad given terrain and FP modifiers?"
02 · How it works

A pipeline, not
a feature list.

Every query travels the same four stages. The output of one is the input to the next — and the result of the whole loop feeds back into the training data for the next iteration. Each stage below maps to a piece of real machinery in the codebase.

i
Stage 01 · Retrieval

RAG over the rulebook

OpenAI's Responses API with native file_search pulls up to 20 relevant chunks from a vector store of the 716-page rulebook. Vision-capable models accept board screenshots alongside text.

Stage 02 · Grounding

Cited answers

Every claim links to the rule section that supports it. Click a citation like A4.34 and the rulebook PDF opens to that exact page in-browser. No hallucinated chapter numbers.

Stage 03 · Evaluation

LLM-as-a-judge

A zero-shot AI judge scores each response Pass / Fail / Needs Review against expected answers. Results are manually reviewed and published — so the accuracy claim is auditable, not asserted.

Stage 04 · Improvement

Curate & retrain

High-quality Q&A pairs are pulled into eval datasets and fine-tuning corpora. Bad answers become test cases. The feedback loop is the whole point.

Curated answers flow back into the retrieval corpus and the eval set. The system gets better at the questions it has already seen, and the eval results document the curve.
